AI and Machine Learning Systems

Throughout my professional time as a software engineer, I have had numerous development phases involving AI or machine learning in one way or another. This page is a breakdown of my most memorable experiences.

Machine Intelligence Systems

As a student, I had coursework that delved into the fascinating world of machine intelligence systems. One standout project involved analyzing a dataset of Titanic passengers, including information such as their names, age, social class, whether they survived or not, and so on. I learned how to write code to make predictions on missing data using previous experience. Here is some of what I accomplished.

- Data Preprocessing: I meticulously cleaned up the data, dropping unnecessary values like ticket numbers and cabin information.

- Feature Engineering: I even considered the impact of family names on survival rates. I predicted that wealthier family names would correlate with a better chance of survival.

- Predictive Modeling: By feeding the data through various machine learning algorithms, I made predictions about the passengers’ chance of survival. One final take I thought was interesting, my final analysis revealed a 2-to-1 ratio of female to male survivors.

Web-Based AI Simulation: "Game of War"

I designed a web-based AI Simulation inspired by cellular automata. Here are the Highlights:

- Cell Classes

- Agressors: These factions preferred attacking other cells.

- Defenders: These cells were more passive, avoided conflict and focused on reinforcing their population

- Competition The goal was simple — become the final team standing. The varying behaviors of cells made this simulation engaging and complex

Golden Gloves Boxing AI

As a VR Game Developer, I tackled the challenge of creating an interactive AI opponent for a poxing game. This AI went through several stages during the development process:

- Standard Game AI: Initially, I used custom, but static logic for the AI. It worked, but we aimed for a more sophisticated approach

- Behavior Tree-Based AI: Next, we implemented a behavior tree that considered inputs like position relative to the player, a status condition comparable to opponent health, and player actions. This allowed the AI to react more intelligently.

- Advanced Behavior System: Our final design surpassed the behavior tree. It made predictions about the player based on their moves, incorporating variable reaction time and decision making.
